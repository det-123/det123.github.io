Engagement models

How we work together.

Engagements differ in structure and level of involvement, but all provide the same level of strategic partnership and executive judgment.

Engagements are scoped based on complexity, urgency, and level of involvement. Most work begins with a short conversation to clarify needs and determine the right model.
Engagement type Overview Common examples
Ad hoc

Targeted guidance on a specific decision or immediate issue, without ongoing involvement.
  • Best for time sensitive questions where experienced HR judgment is needed quickly.
  • Typically limited scope with clear start and finish.
  • Structured as a defined consult, review, or decision support session.
  • Second opinion on a high risk employee relations decision
  • Review of documentation, performance narrative, or termination risk
  • Policy interpretation or compliance risk assessment for a specific situation
  • Manager guidance for a difficult conversation or escalation
Advisory

Ongoing partnership providing continuity, context, and senior HR perspective across evolving priorities.
  • Best for leaders managing complexity over time, not a single discrete issue.
  • Provides consistent thought partnership and decision support across functions and moments.
  • Often structured as a retainer or ongoing advisory cadence.
  • Executive decision support during growth, transition, or integration
  • Ongoing risk governance and escalation framework guidance
  • Organizational design, role clarity, and operating model refinement
  • Leadership coaching and manager effectiveness reinforcement
Fractional HR Leadership

Embedded executive level partnership providing strategic direction, governance oversight, and organizational architecture without adding permanent headcount.
  • Provides consistent executive level guidance and decision support.
  • Balances strategic direction with operational clarity and structure.
  • Structured as a defined term or part time leadership engagement.
  • Acting Head of HR or People leader during transition or growth
  • Organizational structure and governance design
  • Risk and compliance stabilization across multi state environments
  • Building and mentoring internal HR capability
Project based

Defined scope work with clear deliverables, timelines, and outcomes tied to a specific system or initiative.
  • Best for building or improving a specific process, program, or capability.
  • Work is scoped around deliverables and a clear implementation plan.
  • Often structured as a fixed scope engagement with milestones.
  • Policy and compliance framework development
  • Performance management system design and documentation tools
  • Manager training, leader toolkits, and learning programs
  • Investigation support or employee relations process redesign

Not sure which model fits your situation?

Most engagements begin with a conversation to understand context, scope, and priorities. From there, we determine the most appropriate level of support.
