Engagement Models

How we work together.

Organizations engage me in different ways depending on the moment, the level of complexity, and the type of support required. Each model provides a different level of involvement, structure, and continuity.

Ad Hoc Support

Targeted guidance for specific questions, decisions, or immediate challenges. This model is best suited for leaders who need experienced HR perspective on a defined issue without ongoing engagement.

Support may include reviewing documentation, advising on employee relations matters, interpreting policy or compliance questions, or providing decision guidance in time sensitive situations.

Typical use cases: immediate employee issues, risk assessment, policy clarification, documentation review, or second opinion advisory.

Advisory Engagement

Ongoing strategic partnership with leadership teams navigating growth, complexity, or transition. Advisory work provides continuity, context, and consistent senior level HR perspective across evolving business needs.

I work alongside leaders to assess organizational dynamics, design people systems, support decision making, and provide guidance on risk, structure, and workforce strategy over time.

Typical use cases: executive partnership, organizational design, risk management, leadership decision support, and long term HR strategy.

Project Based Work

Defined scope engagements focused on building or improving a specific system, process, or capability. Project work includes clear deliverables, timelines, and measurable outcomes.

Projects often involve designing new infrastructure, implementing programs, or addressing structural challenges that require focused execution.

Typical use cases: policy development, HR infrastructure design, training programs, investigations, compliance frameworks, or organizational initiatives.

Not sure which model fits your situation?

Most engagements begin with a conversation to understand context, scope, and priorities. From there, we determine the most appropriate level of support.
