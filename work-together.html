Ways to Work Together

Find the right fit for what you need.

Not every situation calls for the same level of involvement. Some things need a quick, experienced eye. Others need a steady partner over time. Here's how most engagements take shape.

Scope and pricing are based on what you actually need — complexity, urgency, and level of involvement. Most things start with a short conversation.

One-time consult

Focused guidance on a specific situation, without ongoing involvement. Best when you need experienced HR judgment quickly for a defined issue — a clear start and finish, scoped around a single question or decision.

  • Second opinion on a high-risk ER situation
  • Termination risk review
  • Documentation audit
  • Compliance question
  • Manager coaching session

Ongoing advisory

A continuing partnership — context, continuity, and senior HR perspective as things evolve. Best when you're managing complexity over time, not just one issue. Often structured as a retainer or regular advisory cadence.

  • Decision support during growth or transition
  • Ongoing risk and escalation guidance
  • Org design and operating model work
  • Leadership coaching

Fractional HR leadership

Embedded senior HR leadership — without adding a full-time hire. Provides consistent executive-level HR guidance and direction, balancing strategy with hands-on operational support. Structured as a part-time or defined-term engagement.

  • Acting Head of HR during transition
  • Full HR infrastructure build
  • Compliance stabilization
  • Internal HR team development

Project-based

Defined scope work with clear deliverables tied to a specific system or initiative. Best for building or improving a specific process, program, or capability. Scoped around clear deliverables with milestones and an implementation plan.

  • Policy and compliance framework
  • Performance management system
  • Manager training program
  • Investigation support
  • HRIS implementation
